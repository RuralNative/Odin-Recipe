Adobong Baboy - Sweet and Spicy Version

Adobong baboy (pork adobo) in sweet and spicy version is a popular variation of the classic Filipino adobo dish. This version is characterized by its sweet and tangy flavor, with a hint of spiciness that adds an extra kick to the dish.

Adobong baboy in sweet and spicy version is typically served over steamed rice and garnished with chopped green onions or cilantro. It can be enjoyed as a main course or as a side dish, and is perfect for sharing with family and friends.

Overall, adobong baboy in sweet and spicy version is a delicious and flavorful twist on the classic adobo dish, and is sure to satisfy anyone who loves a little sweetness and heat in their meals.

  • 1 kilogram of pork belly (cut into serving pieces)
  • 1/2 cup of soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup of vinegar
  • 1/4 cup of brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup of banana ketchup
  • 1 head of garlic (peeled and minced)
  • 1-2 pieces of red chili pepper (siling labuyo), chopped (optional)
  • 3-4 pieces of bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon of whole peppercorns
  • 1 cup of water
  • 2 tablespoons of cooking oil

STEPS:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the soy sauce, vinegar, minced garlic, brown sugar, banana ketchup, chopped red chili pepper (if using), bay leaves, and whole peppercorns. Mix well.
  2. Add the pork pieces to the marinade, making sure that each piece is well coated. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes, or preferably overnight.
  3. Heat the cooking oil in a large pan over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the pork pieces (reserve the marinade) and fry until lightly browned on both sides. Remove the pork from the pan and set aside.
  4. In the same pan, pour in the marinade and water. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and add the pork back into the pan. Cover and let the pork simmer for 30-40 minutes, or until the pork is fully cooked and the sauce has thickened.
  5. Once the pork is cooked, transfer it to a serving dish and pour the sauce over the top. Garnish with chopped green onions or cilantro, if desired.
  6. Serve hot with steamed rice and enjoy your sweet and spicy version of adobong baboy!

Note: You can adjust the amount of brown sugar and chili pepper to your personal taste preference. If you want it less spicy, you can skip adding the red chili pepper or remove the seeds.
